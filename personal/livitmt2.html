Hey friends! Here is my proposal to you all for a friendly tournament of loop chess.

  1. Due to troubles in being in more than one Brainking event at a time, we will play the games in the Brainking server, but organize our tournament structure here in my website, as I have done before. (See livitmt, ck1tmt, & egtmt.)

  2. To make it easier for me to keep track of things, I'd prefer that we play only 1 game per opponent. I've done it both ways, & believe me, 1 is easier than 2.

  3. Since we'll have 7 players, this means each of us will play 6 games. Three games will be with white, three with black. Color assignments will be made using the standard charts used by the USCF and other such organizations. Players are placed in a list from 1 to 7, based on current ratings in brainking. Simply stated, if the rank of 2 players are either even-odd, or odd-even, the higher ranked player gets white. If the ranks are both even, or both odd, then the lower ranked player gets white. (Really, it is simple. See list below.)

  4. The player with white is primarily responsible for sending the invitation, though either person may do so.

  5. As to time limit for moves, I'd like to set my personal games at 9 days/move. That's to protect me in case of sickness or unforeseen troubles. However, those who know me, as you all do, know that I tend to move at a regular, much faster pace. So, perhaps each of you could decide what is appropriate for you. Also, set all games for public view and subject to being rated by brainking's system.

  6. As each game is finished, I would like for each player to drop me a note, telling me what happened. You can do this by Brainking's message service or by dropping me an email. However, I will, as time & opportunity permits, go to your profile and see how things are going as well. I will then record the results in the cross-table below.

  7. I feel that we could make the official start for Saturday, February 21, 2004.

  8. If there is anything I've forgotten to mention above, just ask me.

Terry [ ttrotter3@yahoo.com or ttrotter@telesal.net ]
